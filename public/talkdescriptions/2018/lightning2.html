Brewing beer using Android Things by Riaan Cornelius

While some home brewers consider automation to somehow make your brewing less “real”, part of the reason I brew is because I get to make stuff (I also happen to like beer).

In this talk I’ll very briefly go through the beer brewing process and explain what requirements I came up with for my project.

Then I’ll give you a very brief introduction into clean architecture before diving into how I used it to make my Android Things project simple, testable and extendable.

Along the way I’ll show you how you can run your IOT project on an emulator to help with debugging as well as testing.

Your job is not to write code by Arjun Khoosal

You may assume that software engineers are hired to write code. But this definition is too narrow to accommodate software engineers who are good at their job. Good in what sense? Well, the only sense that makes sense: good at helping their company succeed. And for a company to succeed, it must solve problems for its users. And so, we have it: good software engineers are good at solving user problems. Slinging code is but one tool to solve these problems. Getting stuck there does not bode well for your users, for your company, and for you.

Why shipping pet projects is so important by Simon Stewart

This talk is a continuation of a series of talks I gave several years ago on the “importance of pet projects”. The intention is to encourage experimentation and fun outside of the day-to-day project work without promoting burn out. It will also highlight the potential for exposure - both from a marketing point of view and career.

The idea is not to push people to overwork outside of normal office hours, but instead to show the audience that we all have a fantastic skill and can use that to have fun with exposure being a nice potential side effect.
